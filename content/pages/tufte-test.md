## Footnotes as sidenotes

Tufte's style places footnotes in the margin instead of at the bottom of the page[^1]. This keeps the reader's eye on the main text while providing supplementary information nearby[^2].

[^1]: This is a footnote that should appear as a sidenote in the margin on desktop.
[^2]: A second footnote, demonstrating the numbering system.

## Margin notes

Margin notes are unnumbered and appear in the margin without a superscript reference. [note: This is a margin note. It has no number and appears in the margin on desktop, or can be toggled on mobile by clicking the ⊕ symbol.] They are useful for tangential observations that don't warrant a numbered reference.

## Both together

You can use both footnotes[^3] and margin notes in the same section. [note: Like this margin note alongside the footnote.] They each serve a slightly different purpose.
